How It Works

First lets talk about mass. What is mass? The mass of an object is a measure of the object's inertial property, or the amount of matter it contains. That means mass of object is always constant indefendent of place , temperature or any other surrounding factor. So now the question is what is weight? In science and engineering, the weight of an object is the force acting on the object due to gravity of the planet, star or sattelite on which the body is present. Lets see how weight is calculated..
Assume we have a body of mass M on the planet with gravitaitonal force g and we have to calculate its weight W. Therefore wieght of the body on the planet will be

W = M x g
We can see through this that weight depends on the G i.e. gravitational force of the planet.
lets talk about gravitational force of planet whose mass is Mp , radius is R and it have gravitational force g which is calculated as
g = GxM/R2
where G is a Universal Gravitational Constant.
As every planet and star will have different M and different R they all will have different g which will result in different weight of body.
